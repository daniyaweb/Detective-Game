CASE #001: The Stolen Laptop

An interactive detective investigation game built using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Players inspect evidence, interview suspects, and solve a university theft case by identifying the correct culprit.

## About the Project

CASE #001: The Stolen Laptop is a browser-based detective game inspired by classic detective and noir mystery stories.

Professor Owais arrives at his office only to discover that his laptop, containing confidential university records, has been stolen. As the detective, players must investigate the crime scene, inspect clues, question suspects, and use logical reasoning to identify the thief.

---

## Features

- Interactive detective investigation
- Inspect and collect five evidence clues
- Dynamic evidence board
- Detective score system
- Hint system with score deduction
- Collected evidence tracker
- Three suspect interviews
- Final accusation system
- Investigation summary
- Play Again functionality
- Responsive design for desktop, tablet, and mobile devices

---

## How to Play

1. Open the project in your browser.
2. Read the case introduction.
3. Inspect all five clues.
4. Review each clue on the evidence board.
5. Collect every piece of evidence.
6. Interview all suspects.
7. Use hints if necessary (each hint reduces your score).
8. Select the suspect you believe committed the crime.
9. Arrest the suspect.
10. View your final score and investigation summary.

---

## Game Mechanics

- Every collected clue increases your detective score.
- Hints are limited to three uses.
- Each hint deducts five points from your score.
- Players must collect all five clues before making an arrest.
- After the arrest, the game displays:
  - Final score
  - Case outcome
  - Correct culprit
  - Investigation summary
- The game can be restarted using the Play Again button.
