CASE #001

The Stolen Laptop

It was just past 8 AM when Professor Owais unlocked his office door and froze. His desk drawer hung open. His laptop bag sat empty on the chair. The laptop itself, along with confidential university records stored on it, was gone without a trace.

Building logs confirm only three people had access after hours last night — and one of them is not telling the truth.

Five pieces of evidence remain at the scene, waiting to be examined. Read every clue carefully, Detective. The truth is in the details.

Crime Scene Investigation

Security Camera Footage

A figure slips back into the building long after closing time.

The east-wing camera timestamps show a staff member badging back into the building at 9:42 PM — nearly four hours after they claimed to have left for the day. The footage is too dark to confirm a face, but the badge swipe is logged clearly, and the timing places someone exactly where they said they would not be.

Fingerprint on Coffee Cup

A single, still-warm cup sits beside the empty laptop bag.

A partial fingerprint was lifted from the rim of a coffee cup found on Professor Owais's desk. The professor does not drink coffee, and the cup does not match the breakroom set used by lab staff. Whoever sat at this desk last night stayed long enough to finish a drink — meaning they were never in a hurry to leave.

Office Access Log

The electronic door log tells a different story than the suspects do.

The keycard log for Professor Owais's office shows a single entry at 9:40 PM — two minutes before the camera caught someone re-entering the building. The card used belongs to a staff member who swears they were nowhere near the office that night. Logs do not lie. People sometimes do.

Torn Reminder Note

Half a handwritten note, torn and dropped near the filing cabinet.

The surviving fragment reads: "…use the spare key, he won't notice until Monday…" The handwriting is hurried, as if the note was scrawled quickly and torn away before being discarded. It was never meant to be found — and it names the one item every suspect claims they never touched.

Missing Spare Key

A spare office key, dropped and forgotten behind the cabinet.

The department keeps exactly one spare key to Professor Owais's office, normally signed out only by teaching staff for grading access. That key was found wedged behind the filing cabinet — far from its usual place on the front-desk hook, as if someone needed it returned quietly, and quickly.
